Breakfast

  • Avocado Toast

    Simple and healthy, topped with fresh avocado and spices.

    Ingredients:
    • 1 to 2 avocados
    • Balsamic vinegar
    • Dijon mustard
    • Virgin olive oil
    • 1 garlic clove
    • Any bread
    Directions:
    1. Make a vinaigrette using balsamic vinegar, virgin olive oil, dijon mustard, and chopped fresh garlic.
    2. Take a few tablespoons of olive oil and add some mustard and vinegar. Beat with a fork until creamy.
    3. Add chopped garlic, then mix.
    4. Slice bread and toast it.
    5. Take your avocado and empty it into a bowl, minus the pit and skin.
    6. Use a fork to mash it up a bit, leaving it chunky, yet spreadable.
    7. Take the vinaigrette and add it to the avocado and mix a little bit.
    8. Once the toast is ready, scoop some of the avocado mixture onto it.
    9. Optionally, top it with chopped tomatoes and salt & pepper for extra flavor.

  • Blueberry Muffins

    Soft, fluffy muffins bursting with blueberries.

    Ingredients:
    • 1 cup oats
    • 1 cup buttermilk
    • 1 cup white wheat flour
    • 1 teaspoon baking powder
    • ½ teaspoon baking soda
    • ½ teaspoon salt (iodized only)
    • ¾ cup brown sugar
    • 1 egg
    • ¼ cup butter, melted
    • 1 cup fresh blueberries, drained well (or 1 cup frozen blueberries, thawed and drained, tossed in a tablespoon of flour to prevent sinking)
    Directions:
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
    2. Mix oats and buttermilk in a small bowl, and set aside.
    3. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and brown sugar.
    4. Add the egg and melted butter to the oat mixture, then stir in the dry ingredients until just moistened.
    5. Gently fold in the blueberries.
    6. Spoon batter into a muffin pan, filling each cup about three-quarters full.
    7. Bake for 15-20 minutes. Enjoy as is or with toppings.

Lunch

  • Grilled Cheese Sandwich

    A classic sandwich with gooey melted cheese.

    Ingredients:
    • 2 slices bread
    • 2 tablespoons butter, divided
    • 2 slices American cheese
    Directions:
    1. Heat a small skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Spread a thin layer of butter on one side of each bread slice.
    3. Place one slice of bread, buttered-side-down, in the hot skillet.
    4. Quickly add both slices of cheese on top, then cover with the second slice of bread, buttered-side-up.
    5. Once the first side is golden brown, flip the sandwich to brown the other side.
    6. Remove from heat and let it cool for 2-3 minutes before serving.

  • Caesar Salad

    Makes 6 servings and ⅔ cup salad dressing. Preparation time: 15 minutes.

    Ingredients:
    • Salad Dressing:
      • 3 tbsp light Mayonnaise
      • 1 clove garlic, minced
      • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
      • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
      • 1 tbsp lemon juice
      • 1 dash hot pepper sauce
      • 1 dash Worcestershire sauce
      • ¼ cup olive oil
      • 2 tbsp water
    • Salad:
      • 8 cups romaine lettuce leaves, torn
      • 1½ cup toasted bread croutons
      • 1 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
    Directions:
    1. Dressing: In a bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, garlic, vinegar, mustard, lemon juice, hot pepper sauce, and Worcestershire sauce.
    2. Slowly whisk in olive oil and water until the dressing is smooth and well combined.
    3. Salad: In a large bowl, combine the torn lettuce, croutons, and Parmesan cheese.
    4. Toss with about ⅓ cup of the dressing (half of the total).
    5. Store the leftover dressing in the fridge for another use.
    6. For a single serving, use approximately 1 cup of lettuce, ¼ cup of croutons, 1 tablespoon of dressing, and 1 teaspoon of Parmesan cheese.

Dinner

  • Spaghetti Bolognese

    A hearty Italian dish with a savory meat sauce over pasta.

    Ingredients:
    • 1 package of spaghetti
    • 2 peppers
    • 2 medium tomatoes, cooked
    • 1 garlic clove
    • 1 carrot
    • ½ onion
    • ½ kg ground beef
    • 1 pinch of basil
    • ½ cup Parmesan cheese
    • Oil, as necessary
    • Salt to taste
    Directions:
    1. Cook the spaghetti in 1 liter of water with the peppers, a little oil, and salt. Once cooked, drain the pasta.
    2. Blend the cooked, peeled tomatoes with garlic and a bit of water until smooth.
    3. Finely chop the carrot and onion, then sauté them with a pinch of salt. Once the vegetables are softened, add the ground meat, blended tomatoes, and basil. Stir well and cook over medium heat until the tomato liquid has evaporated.
    4. Once the meat is fully cooked, serve the pasta and top with the meat mixture. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese before serving.

  • Grilled Salmon

    Delicious salmon fillet, perfectly grilled and seasoned.

    Ingredients:
    • 1 ½ lb. salmon, defrosted
    • 2 tbsp. olive oil
    • ½ tsp. dried dill or basil
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1 tbsp. lemon juice
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    Directions:
    1. Preheat the grill. In a small bowl, mix together olive oil, herbs, garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
    2. Brush the salmon with the herb mixture using a pastry brush.
    3. Grill the salmon until fully cooked (it's done when it flakes easily with a fork). Serve hot, either plain or with rice or other seafood.

Desserts

  • Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

    These homemade chocolate chip cookies are soft, chewy, and loaded with chocolate chips. They're easy to make, with no mixer needed!

    Ingredients:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1 teaspoon sea salt
    • ¾ cup unsalted butter, 1½ sticks, melted and cooled slightly
    • ½ cup packed brown sugar
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • 1 large egg
    • 1 large egg yolk
    • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
    • 1 heaping cup dark chocolate chips
    Directions:
    1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
    2. In a large bowl, whisk the melted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together. Add the egg and egg yolk, whisking until smooth and no egg streaks remain. Stir in the vanilla.
    3. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients with a spatula. Once mostly combined, fold in the chocolate chips until a soft dough forms and no dry flour is left.
    4. Cover and refrigerate the dough for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 days. Shorter chilling times will result in flatter cookies, while longer chilling (2+ hours) will give you thicker cookies with less spread.

  • Strawberry Shortcake

    Light and fluffy cake with fresh strawberries and cream.

    Ingredients:
    • 2 cups flour
    • 2 teaspoons baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/3 cup butter, cold and cut into chunks
    • 7/8 cup milk
    • Fresh Strawberries
    Directions:
    1. Sift together the dry ingredients.
    2. Use your fingertips or a pastry cutter to work the cold butter into the flour mixture, just like making pie crust or biscuits. Keep everything cold and handle quickly.
    3. Gradually add the milk and stir only enough to form a soft dough.
    4. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and roll it out to about ½" thick.
    5. Cut the dough into 3-inch squares, use a 2½" round cookie cutter, or cut large 6-7 inch circles for bigger shortcakes.
    6. Bake in a preheated oven at 450°F. The baking time will vary depending on the size of the shortcakes. Smaller ones may bake in 10 minutes or less, while larger ones may take 15-20 minutes. Keep an eye on them to prevent burning.
    7. Once baked, remove from the oven and let cool on a wire rack. Split them in half as soon as they're cool enough to handle.
    8. Serve with whipped cream or pastry cream, topped with fresh strawberries, strawberry sauce, and chocolate shavings. Dust with powdered sugar and garnish with fresh mint.
